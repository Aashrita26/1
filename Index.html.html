AI Against The World!

We have all seen the benefits of AI, how it helps us in anything we find difficult or have trouble understanding, even if its trying to find a name for your cat. But not everything about it is beneficial.

We have all heard about the idea of Robots taking over the world, right? Well what if that wasn't meant for robots but instead something more well developed and more advanced. Maybe AI

While AI is expected to replace millions of jobs, that could have been there for people in need for a regular wage to support families. AI has caused thousands of layoffs and decreased oppurtunities for even well educated citizens.
Other than just jobs, it is impacting school-going children, who rely on AI to complete their homework and other tasks making them lazy when them need to use a little bit of thinking power. Researches have proved the impacts of AI on young minds. " Too Much Information: Constant exposure to AI-powered content can overload youth with information, causing too much information. The fast pace of AI-based tools can lower attention spans and makes it harder for youth to think deeply, analyze critically, and solve problems. ● Weakened Memory: AI’s ability to give instant answers might stop youth from remembering information and improving their critical thinking. Instead of actively recalling knowledge, students may become inactive users of information, weakening their memory skills. ● Lowered Creativity: AI tools often work based on fixed rules, which may limit young people’s creativity by offering only certain solutions. This could reduce their ability to think outside the box and come up with original ideas, important for solving problems and academic success. Emotional and Mental Effects of AI on Youth The strong presence of AI in daily life has noticeable effects on the emotional well-being of young people. From the use of AI in social media to video games, AI is a big part of emotional experiences. "

AI isn't someone you should get personal with

AI is not your therapist: AI is not a trained human therapist. It doesn’t truly understand emotions, trauma, or complex mental health conditions like a licensed psychologist does. It also can’t pick up on subtle warning signs the way a human can, especially in serious situations like depression or crisis. In those cases, relying only on AI can delay someone from getting real help, which can make things worse. There’s also the risk of people becoming too dependent on it. If someone starts using AI as their only support system, they might avoid real relationships or professional care, which are actually really important for their mental health.                 AI is not someone to share passwords with You should never tell an AI your actual password, even if it feels private or harmless. Anything you type could potentially be stored, reviewed, or exposed in ways you don’t expect. It’s the same rule as with strangers online: if it unlocks your account, it should stay completely secret. Even asking an AI something like “is this a good password?” can be unsafe if you include the real one. That’s basically revealing it. Once a password is shared, it’s no longer secure. There’s also a smaller but still important issue with asking AI to create your password. It’s not always dangerous, but it’s not the best idea either. AI-generated passwords might follow patterns, and if lots of people use similar prompts, those patterns could become predictable. Hackers look for patterns like that. Instead of using AI to generate random password you could use more secure and safe apps like Password1 or Bitwarden ,which genuinely creates strong passwords. 10 things not to tell AI
